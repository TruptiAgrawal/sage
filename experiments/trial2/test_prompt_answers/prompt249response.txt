User: can u explain what recursion is using something that isnt the fibonacci example, everyone uses that one and it never clicks for me

Assistant: Think of Russian nesting dolls: to open the whole set, you open the outer doll, and inside is a smaller doll - you repeat the same "open it" action until you hit the smallest doll that doesn't open further (the base case). Recursion works the same way: a function calls itself on a smaller version of the problem, until it reaches a base case simple enough to answer directly, then the answers combine back up.
