Assistant: Data mining is the process of discovering patterns, correlations, and insights from large datasets using statistical and computational techniques. Kinds of data mined: relational databases, data warehouses, transactional data, text, multimedia, spatial, and web data. Kinds of patterns: classification, clustering, association rules, sequential patterns, and outlier/anomaly detection. Technologies used: statistics, machine learning, database systems, and visualization tools. Applications: market basket analysis, fraud detection, customer segmentation, healthcare diagnostics, and recommendation systems. Major issues: handling noisy/incomplete data, scalability to massive datasets, privacy concerns, and interpretability of complex patterns found.
