User: How do you make coffee?

Assistant: Making great coffee is both an art and a science. Here's a comprehensive guide:

**Step 1: Choose Your Equipment**
The equipment you use significantly affects the final product. Options include:
- Pour over (cone or flat-bottom): Simple, controllable, produces clean cups
- French press: Full-bodied, rich flavor, but sediment in cup
- Espresso machine: High pressure extraction, concentrated shots
- Moka pot: Stovetop, produces strong coffee
- Aeropress: Quick, clean, versatile
- Turkish coffee pot: Traditional, very fine powder

Each method has different extraction times and temperatures that affect flavor.

**Step 2: Select and Grind Beans**
Coffee quality starts with beans. Look for specialty-grade beans with a roast date within 2-4 weeks. Grind size matters tremendously—it affects how quickly water extracts compounds from the beans:
- Coarse (French press): Large particles, longer steeping
- Medium (pour over): Balanced extraction
- Fine (espresso): Tight packing, quick high-pressure extraction

Grind just before brewing; grinding exposes surface area and flavors dissipate quickly.

**Step 3: Control Water Temperature**
Ideal brewing temperature is 195-205°F (90-96°C). Water that's too hot over-extracts (bitter), too cool under-extracts (sour). If boiling water, let it sit 30 seconds after boiling.

**Step 4: Maintain Ratios**
Coffee-to-water ratio dramatically affects strength and flavor. A starting point is 1:16 (1g coffee to 16g water), but adjust to taste. Some prefer stronger (1:12), others lighter (1:18).

**Step 5: Brew**
Follow your equipment's instructions for timing and technique. Most methods take 3-5 minutes.

**Step 6: Serve Immediately**
Coffee is best consumed fresh. Holding coffee hot for extended periods degrades flavor compounds.

**For Best Results:**
- Use fresh, quality beans
- Grind immediately before brewing
- Use filtered water
- Maintain consistent temperature
- Experiment with ratios and grind sizes
- Keep equipment clean
