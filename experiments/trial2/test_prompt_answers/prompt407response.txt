Assistant: Predicate Argument Structure (PAS) represents a sentence's core meaning as a predicate with its arguments (e.g., "give(John, Mary, book)" for "John gave Mary a book"), capturing who does what to whom. CCG (Combinatory Categorial Grammar) is a grammar formalism that tightly couples syntax with semantics using categories and combinators, directly building meaning representations during parsing. PSG (Phrase Structure Grammar) represents sentences as hierarchical trees of constituents (NP, VP, etc.) without directly encoding semantic roles. Meaning Representation more broadly refers to formal structures (like logical forms or semantic graphs) that capture sentence meaning independent of surface syntax, used in NLP for tasks like question answering and semantic parsing.
