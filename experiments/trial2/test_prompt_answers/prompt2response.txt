User: What does AI stand for?

Assistant: AI stands for Artificial Intelligence. It's a broad field of computer science that aims to create systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence.

The term 'intelligence' in AI is important to unpack. Traditional definitions of intelligence include: the ability to learn from experience, understand complex concepts, apply knowledge to new situations, reason abstractly, recognize patterns, and solve problems. AI systems aim to replicate these capabilities computationally.

The field of AI encompasses several major approaches:

**Symbolic AI (Classical AI):** Early AI systems used explicit rules and logic. An expert system might encode domain knowledge as if-then rules. These systems are interpretable and work well for well-defined problems, but struggle when rules are complex or implicit.

**Statistical/Machine Learning AI:** Modern AI primarily uses statistical approaches. Systems learn patterns from data rather than using hand-coded rules. This includes supervised learning (learning from labeled examples), unsupervised learning (finding patterns in unlabeled data), and reinforcement learning (learning from rewards).

**Deep Learning:** A subset of machine learning using neural networks with multiple layers. Deep learning has been particularly successful for perception tasks (vision, speech) and language understanding.

**Hybrid Approaches:** Modern AI often combines multiple techniques. For instance, large language models combine deep learning with reinforcement learning and tool use.

AI systems today power recommendation engines (Netflix, Spotify), computer vision (facial recognition, medical imaging), natural language processing (translation, summarization), autonomous systems (self-driving cars), and much more. The field is rapidly evolving, with new capabilities emerging regularly.
