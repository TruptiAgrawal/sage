User: Who invented the internet?

Assistant: The Internet wasn't invented by a single person but developed through collaborative efforts across decades. Here are the key figures and moments:

**Early Foundations (1960s):**
J.C.R. Licklider envisioned networked computing in his 1960 paper "Man-Computer Symbiosis." He imagined computers working together as a network, which was revolutionary at the time.

**ARPANET (1969):**
The Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) funded the ARPANET project. Key players included:
- Bob Taylor: ARPA program manager who championed the idea
- Larry Roberts: Oversaw the project's development
- Bob Kahn and Vint Cerf: Developed TCP/IP protocols that became fundamental

**TCP/IP Protocols (1970s):**
Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn developed the Transmission Control Protocol (TCP) and Internet Protocol (IP). These protocols solved the fundamental problem of routing data packets across different networks. Their work was crucial because it allowed different networks to interconnect—the defining feature of the Internet.

**Email and Early Applications (1970s):**
Ray Tomlinson sent the first network email in 1971. Email became the killer app that showed the value of networking.

**Domain Name System (1983):**
Paul Mockapetris developed the DNS system, which translated domain names to IP addresses, making the Internet human-friendly.

**World Wide Web (1989-1991):**
Tim Berners-Lee, working at CERN, invented the World Wide Web—the system of hyperlinked documents and the HTTP protocol. While the Internet provided connectivity, the Web provided an easy way for people to share and access information.

**Key Infrastructure:**
- Jon Postel: Managed IANA (IP address allocation)
- Steve Crocker: Developed the RFC process for protocol standardization
- Many others: Thousands of engineers, academics, and programmers built routers, protocols, and software

**Why Multiple Inventors:**
The Internet emerged from solving specific technical problems iteratively. No single leap created it—rather, each solution (packet switching, TCP/IP, DNS, protocols for applications) built on previous work. Different parts were developed semi-independently then integrated.

**Timeline:**
1969: ARPANET connects 4 computers
1974: TCP/IP protocols published
1981: ARPANET has 213 hosts
1983: TCP/IP becomes standard
1989: Web invented
1991: Web becomes public
1995: Commercial Internet boom
2000s-present: Internet becomes global infrastructure

The Internet represents perhaps humanity's greatest collaborative technical achievement, built by thousands of people over decades.
